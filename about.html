About Me

I work at the intersection of AI, data science, and engineering, with a focus on understanding complex time-series and biosignal data. Recently, I’ve been exploring sleep research, EEG analysis, and emerging healthtech applications, aiming to build tools that make physiological data more interpretable and accessible.

My background includes a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Engineering and a Master’s degree in Business Analytics, giving me a foundation in both technical development and analytical problem-solving. I enjoy working with Python, machine learning, and signal processing methods to uncover patterns in noisy real-world data.

I’m particularly interested in how AI can support insights in sleep, neurodata, and digital health, and I’m gradually expanding my work toward open-source projects that bridge data science with human physiology.
